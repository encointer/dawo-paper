## Summary

**Community Growth:** Active members increased from 10 to 45, primarily young business owners aged 25-35.

**Voting Dynamics:** Governance operates on a one-person, one-vote basis; understanding of voting mechanics is still developing.

**Swap Challenges:** Business owners find Panuk swaps frustrating due to slow processing and navigation issues.

**Proposal Process:** Community leadership initiates proposals, typically getting near 100% approval with minimal dissent.

**Improvement Goals:** Leadership aims to speed up proposal enactment and transaction processes, currently exceeding 10-12 days.

**Communication Channels:** Proposals discussed mainly in a WhatsApp group, fostering polite but open engagement among members.

## Notes

### Community Profile and Growth

- The community has grown from about **10 to 45 active voting members**, primarily young business owners and students, with most aged around **25 to 35 years** (04:17).
- The majority are **male**, with an estimated **60% male to 40% female ratio** (05:54).
- Community members include **business owners such as barbers, farmers, tailors, shopkeepers, restaurant owners, and telecom operators**, reflecting the initial profile described in the launch blog (06:49).
- Three typical participants described: a **32-year-old married business owner and student**, a **25-year-old male farmer with a graduate degree**, and a **23-year-old female entrepreneur managing a grocery and fashion shop** (09:21).
- Some members stopped participating due to **lack of motivation, difficulty accepting change, or technical issues** like poor device or network connectivity (13:19, 14:53).

### Democracy and Governance Features

- Community members initially reacted with **confusion but eventual acceptance** when the nominal income was adjusted from **100 to 1 Panuk**, once the value equivalence was explained (17:47).
- The **governance system is close to one person, one vote**, weighted by reputation, but many community members, including leadership, did **not fully understand the voting power mechanics** until recently (26:09).
- Democracy is viewed as a **necessary transparency measure** rather than a highly engaging feature; voting activity remains steady but not growing despite community size increase (28:25).
- Voting education happens during **community meetups and through peer explanations**, with new members sometimes unable to vote due to insufficient Panuk balance or reputation (31:24).
- Proposals for spending KSM funds are mostly initiated by **community leadership (mainly Mubarak)**, shared as screenshots in a **WhatsApp group of about 88 members**, and generally receive **near 100% approval with little controversy** (22:14, 24:10, 36:44).
- Lack of "no" votes is attributed to the community's **small size and harmonious culture**, where concerns are raised early and resolved before voting (39:55).

### Swap Option Challenges and Business Impact

- The swap option was introduced to address businesses' **difficulty accepting and converting Panuk currency**, but it currently causes frustration due to **slow and inconvenient conversion processes** (42:26).
- Business owners remain hesitant to accept Panuk because it is **new and less liquid compared to traditional currency or Bitcoin**, and the swap process is perceived as slow and unreliable (43:50).
- Zainab shared her personal experience of having **about 60 Panuk pending conversion for over a month**, which is discouraging for her and other business owners (45:07).
- The main pain point the swap option could solve is enabling **direct, instant currency swaps within the app**, allowing business owners to control their funds and avoid lengthy waits (46:39).
- Current swap issues include **inconvenient navigation to the swap feature, minimum top-up limits, and restrictions on inactive business wallet addresses** (48:54).
- Businesses need a **seamless, fast conversion process within days to keep liquidity flowing and restock inventory**, which is critical for sustained participation (53:17).

### Process Improvements and Future Priorities

- The community leadership aims to improve the **democracy process speed**, especially the **time it takes for proposals to be enacted and swaps to complete**, which currently can take **10 to 12 days or more** (55:32).
- Mubarak emphasized the importance of reducing the **attestation phase duration** to make governance and swap proposals more practical and business-friendly (55:32).
- Malik committed to **funding pending transactions immediately after the call** and to working on a process for converting pending Panuk faster in future meetings (56:34, 58:45).
- Scheduling follow-up meetings is planned, with a tentative slot on **Friday 4-5 PM local time**, to focus on resolving pending currency conversions and improving swap mechanisms (59:52, 01:00:38).

### Community Communication and Transparency

- Communication around proposals is done mainly via a **WhatsApp group containing 88 members**, which includes current, past, and interested community members (24:10).
- Proposals are **shared with screenshots and discussed during meetups**, with voting taking place mostly after community discussions (37:30).
- Community members react with **questions or emojis but rarely express opposition in the group chat**, indicating a culture of open but polite engagement (38:52).
- The leadership encourages **peer-to-peer explanation of governance features** to ensure understanding and collective ownership of the process (33:48).

### Overall Strategic Insights

- The community is **still early in its adoption curve** for blockchain governance and currency swapping, with **technical literacy and process speed as key barriers** to deeper engagement (26:09, 55:32).
- The current governance model prioritizes **transparency and collective decision-making**, but its impact is limited by **lack of enthusiasm and slow procedural timelines** (29:18).
- Swap functionality has **high potential to improve business participation** if the process becomes faster and more intuitive, directly impacting liquidity and community growth (46:39).
- Leadership is aware of these challenges and is actively working on **process improvements and education efforts** to increase community confidence and operational efficiency (31:24, 56:34).
