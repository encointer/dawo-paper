# Nyota Leadership Interview Guide

*Semi-structured | ~45 minutes | Basis for scientific publication*

---

## Opening (~2 min)

*This interview is the basis for a scientific publication. I may ask about things we've touched on before — please just answer naturally. We'll focus on the period from **early 2025** onwards, when the governance features were introduced.*

## 1. Community Overview (~7 min)

**You have seven business groups and around 60 active members in Mburahati: Do all groups engage equally in community life, or is activity concentrated in certain ones?**

- Arrange people that are dealing with the same stuff (economically).
- All groups are participating. Not a 100% in each group.

**Which are active now / Which are not active?**

Nature of Business makes about the activities.

Mburahati doesn't have rich people. Low earners.

Tailoring is still the best until now. Many ladies can't go without a new dress in 2 weeks (activities: b-day, marriage party…). Hard to compare with the ones dealing with daily use stuff. They can get customers, but it's not the same.

Goes on to explain about dresses: How the culture is to bring fabric and designs to contribute to the activity.

**Are there people who tried Nyota and stopped — and do you know why?**

Yes, a lot of people that tried. Also very good members. Different reasons:

- Very good phone and lost it
- Life changes: Work far from Mburahati. Ali sees them at night
- Not used to smartphones / apps.
- Devil thing, don't understand / ignorance. They come from the air. How is this possible?

**We know participation dropped during the election period due to curfews, and that older members have become less regular while younger members are now stepping up. How has that generational shift changed the community dynamic?**

It has been hard. First time such an experience. Change them in terms of life. Lost a lot reputables.

It's too hard to get people. People don't want to come out. People don't want to be seen in such mobilizations and the economy is more challenging.

Mostly the people participated have to find food for families.

Youth is now more engaged. They have time and more savvy. It gave them a new way of engagement. New leaders that are working with Nyota.

**Describe 3 completely different group of people that participate in Nyota. What is their background / age / profession?**

Yes. No names needed.

- Housewives. Mostly from home economy. Security for them.
- Ladies that do business (small business). Daily economic stuff e.g. tailors. Some have shops and others that do rice, cooking oil. Around 20-35 years old. Could be housewives.
- Students, University students. In different institutions (big and small universities). 18-25 years old. Most active groups. Challenge, if they have classes. They help organize the attestation.

## 2. The Democracy Feature — Spend & Proposals (~15 min)

**Do you remember the first time you used the voting feature? What was the proposal, and how did the community react?**

Yes, I think it was spend KSM. It was with the business groups. Mostly members in business groups. We used to engage them in meetings. 2 up to 4 business groups to discuss how to use democracy.

First was a petition to ask the community about things.

Asked the community if they could accept them to get the capital injection funds. Showing the people how to interact. Very positive. Find a way to explain what the group is doing. Thanks to the votes they knew what was happening.

**You used petitions early on — what was their purpose, and why did you move away from them?**

The rate of spending was there, now it's not anymore. Things have changed. Nyota swapping easily. Jukumu uses KSM spending mainly.

**Follow-up: Looking back, do you think the petitions helped the community understand what governance was for?**

Yes, yes very true. It still helps. Transparency, you can't vote on things you don't know. Where to meet could be used also.

**How did members learn to vote? Walk me through how that education happened.**

Before, Jukumu meets with business groups and explains. Now things have changed. Leaders train.

Leaders come from different streets. 3 streets in Mburahati. Each leader has 10-15 members to lead.

Vote around Jan/Feb used two attestations. We want to have new leaders. Give members the chance to nominate. Sent names of people. Mentioned the names to the members.

=> Thought about using democracy feature. But most people are still not trained to do so.

**Who typically submits KSM spend proposals, and why those people specifically? Has anyone ever wanted to submit a proposal but didn't know how, or felt it wasn't their place?**

Only Jukumu did that. Trained a group of 15 on how to use.

First thing to do: screenshot made and share in the WhatsApp group. Because there are members / customers and so on.

You get the votes from people that use your service. E.g., the teacher will get votes from parents whose kids are in school. Leaders are still training.

Leaders help members to create more transactions in the community. So the community reserve spendings create value in the community.

## 3. Swap Options (~15 min)

**Before swap options existed, how did businesses handle accumulating too much Nyota?**

It was Spend KSM. By reports from Encointer they could see. Jukumu would track and support the members. E.g. 400 Nyota collected. They'll call the person. They ask them and support. Jukumu would take maybe 50%. This is what they did last year. Support businesses that make a lot of revenue.

**What changed when businesses got the ability to swap Nyota to USDC directly in the app?**

So much. Even the value of Nyota has changed. Before people would spend Nyota with no reason (e.g. just sending 10 Nyota or instead of 10 send 15). But now they can change it easily into USDC and this doesn't happen. Also leaders collect Nyota as well. So from Attestations alone, you don't have enough => Business.

Example of a person that loaned Nyota + interest. Loan 30 Nyota and get 35 Nyota back.

**Sometimes people don't have money but they have Nyota!!!!**

**Every swap option requires a community vote — so in practice, how does a business come to receive one? Is there an informal process before it goes on-chain? Have there been situations where a business had a lot of surplus Nyota but a swap option wasn't put forward for them — what happened there?**

WhatsApp Group. Is like a Bazaar. ~120 members. It's like a market group.

Only the ones who understand my business will support me. (The first people I reach out to are my customers). Screenshot and share with the group. Share your number.

**We observe 100% approval rates. Has there ever been a proposal you personally had doubts about but voted for anyway?**

They are still training people to say Nay. => As **security**!! When they grow. They have to come to Jukumu to be trained. For the future.

Voters: the ones that made business with me.

Predicts: It will change. More NOs, when they grow. They can say let's accumulate the money for 1 year e.g. => Open a Nyota store.

**Before a proposal goes on-chain, is there discussion or negotiation within the community — and if so, who is part of that conversation?**

Already addressed.

**Has anyone come to you after a vote saying they weren't really sure about it?**

Already addressed.

**How did you explain the swap option process to the community — and what was their reaction?**

Already addressed.

**Where does it still break down or create friction for members?**

Ali: Challenges is not a no. Right now, too hard to understand. How to engage members, and governments' support.

**To see the positive things. Regulations are still not friendly in Tanzania. Trying to register the community group. To make it more popular in a very good way! Guidance for the country for the regulation.**
